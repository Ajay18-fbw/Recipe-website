Paneer Tikka

Smoky, spiced, and perfectly charred paneer cubes — the ultimate Indian vegetarian starter!

Prep Time
10 mins
Marinate
30 mins
Cook Time
15 mins
Servings
4 people
Difficulty
Easy

Ingredients

  • 400g paneer, cut into 1.5-inch cubes
  • 1 large capsicum (any color), cut into squares
  • 1 large onion, cut into petals
  • 1 cup thick yogurt
  • 2 tbsp tikka masala or tandoori masala
  • 1 tsp red chilli powder
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp oil (for marinade)
  • Salt to taste
  • Chaat masala for sprinkling
  • Mint chutney to serve
  • Wooden or metal skewers

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. 1
    Make the marinade: In a large bowl, mix together yogurt, tikka masala, red chilli powder, cumin powder, lemon juice, oil, and salt. Whisk until smooth and creamy.
  2. 2
    Marinate paneer and veggies: Add paneer cubes, capsicum, and onion petals to the marinade. Gently mix until everything is well coated. Cover the bowl with cling wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.
  3. 3
    Thread the skewers: Thread the marinated paneer, capsicum, and onion alternately onto skewers. Keep them close together but not squished.
  4. 4
    Cook the tikka: Heat a grill pan or tawa over high heat and brush with a little oil. Place the skewers on the pan and cook for 3–4 minutes on each side, turning gently, until you get nice char marks.
  5. 5
    Oven method (alternative): Place skewers on a baking tray. Bake at 220°C for 12–15 minutes, then switch to the grill/broiler setting for 3–4 minutes to get charred edges.
  6. 6
    Serve hot: Remove from skewers (or keep on for presentation). Sprinkle chaat masala on top. Serve with fresh mint chutney and sliced onion rings. Squeeze a little lemon juice before eating. Enjoy!
